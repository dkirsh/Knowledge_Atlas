Knowledge Atlas catalogues ~ 1,400 peer-reviewed studies on architecture, environment, and cognition. If you know of a study we should add, tell us about it — usually takes under a minute.

Why it's useful

Every paper we index strengthens the Atlas in three ways:

  • Better coverage: a topic bundle with six papers is more defensible than one with three.
  • Better contradictions: papers that disagree with existing evidence sharpen the picture.
  • Better synthesis: more primary-source breadth lets the system generate more honest mechanism and theory summaries.

You don't need to tag anything, classify anything, or format the metadata. Drop the PDF or the citation and we'll take it from there.

What happens next

Each submission is screened automatically the moment it lands. Accepted papers and edge cases enter the review queue (a few days). Off-topic submissions and duplicates are caught up-front and shown here so you know right away. If we don't add it, we'll tell you why.
